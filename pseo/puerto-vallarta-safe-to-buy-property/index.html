Is Puerto Vallarta Safe to Buy Property? A Local Expert's Perspective

Poncho Davalos AMPI Associate, Riviera Nayarit
March 2026 — Updated: 2026-04-01

Is Puerto Vallarta Really Safe for Foreign Property Buyers?

If you're reading this, chances are you've already fallen in love with Puerto Vallarta — the sunsets over Banderas Bay, the warmth of the people, the incredible food, and that feeling you get walking down the Malecón as the evening breeze rolls in. But before you make the leap and invest in property here, you need to answer one critical question: Is it safe?

As Poncho Dávalos, I've spent over a decade helping American, Canadian, and international buyers find their dream homes and investment properties in Puerto Vallarta. I've walked this path with hundreds of families, and I want to give you the honest, data-backed answer you deserve.

What the Data Actually Says About Safety in Puerto Vallarta

Let's start with the numbers, because feelings and headlines don't tell the whole story — data does. According to the ENSU (Encuesta Nacional de Seguridad Pública Urbana) published by INEGI in January 2026, Puerto Vallarta has an insecurity perception of just 32%. To put that in perspective:

  • Cancún: 81% insecurity perception
  • Guadalajara: 73.9% insecurity perception
  • Querétaro: 48% insecurity perception
  • Puerto Vallarta: 32% insecurity perception

That's not a small difference — it's a chasm. Puerto Vallarta doesn't just edge out other destinations; it dramatically outperforms them in perceived safety. And perception matters because it reflects the lived experience of residents, including the thousands of expats who call this city home year-round.

Why Is Puerto Vallarta So Much Safer?

Several factors contribute to Puerto Vallarta's exceptional safety record. First, the city's economy is built almost entirely on tourism and hospitality. When your livelihood depends on visitors feeling welcome and secure, the entire community has a vested interest in maintaining safety. The local government, business owners, and residents all understand that safety is Puerto Vallarta's most valuable asset.

Second, the municipal government has invested significantly in public safety infrastructure. This includes dedicated tourist police units who patrol popular areas like the Malecón, Zona Romántica, and Marina Vallarta. Surveillance systems cover key intersections and public spaces, and community policing programs build trust between law enforcement and residential neighborhoods.

Third, Puerto Vallarta benefits from its geographic position. Nestled between the Sierra Madre mountains and Banderas Bay, the city has natural boundaries that contribute to a more controlled and manageable urban environment compared to sprawling metropolitan areas.

The Thriving Expatriate Community: Living Proof of Safety

Numbers are important, but nothing speaks louder than the thousands of Americans and Canadians who have chosen to make Puerto Vallarta their home. The expat community here isn't just surviving — it's thriving. You'll find active social clubs, volunteer organizations, business networks, and cultural groups that bring together people from all over the world.

In my years as a real estate advisor, I've had the privilege of helping families from Texas, California, Alberta, Ontario, and beyond find their perfect property here. Many of them arrived with the same concerns you might have right now. Today, they're some of Puerto Vallarta's most passionate advocates. They walk to dinner in the evening, explore local markets on weekends, and live their lives with a freedom and peace of mind that many say they didn't even have back home.

A Personal Story

I remember helping a couple from Minnesota who were terrified about buying in Mexico. They'd read every scary headline and almost talked themselves out of it. I invited them to spend a week here — not in a resort, but in a real neighborhood. They walked the streets, talked to other expats, visited the local tianguis (market), and ate at family-run restaurants. By day three, they asked me to start looking at condos. They've been here five years now, and they tell me it was the best decision they ever made.

Economic Stability: The Foundation of a Safe Investment

Safety isn't just about physical security — it's about economic stability too. Puerto Vallarta's economy is remarkably resilient, anchored by a tourism sector that shows no signs of slowing down. Consider these numbers:

  • 82.7% hotel occupancy rate — one of the highest in Mexico, indicating strong and consistent tourist demand
  • 166% average ROI on vacation rental properties, meaning your investment works hard for you
  • +7.9% annual property appreciation, steadily building your equity over time
  • Growing international airport with direct flights from 30+ cities in the US and Canada

When the economy is healthy, communities are stable. When communities are stable, property values are protected. It's a virtuous cycle that makes Puerto Vallarta one of the most secure real estate markets in all of Mexico.

Legal Protections for Foreign Buyers

Beyond physical safety, you need to know that your investment is legally protected. Mexico has a well-established legal framework for foreign property ownership:

  • Fideicomiso (Bank Trust): For properties within 50 km of the coast, a Mexican bank holds the title in trust on your behalf, giving you full ownership rights including the right to sell, rent, remodel, or pass the property to your heirs.
  • Notary Public System: Every real estate transaction in Mexico must be certified by a notary public — a government-appointed legal official who verifies the legitimacy of the sale, ensures clear title, and registers the transaction with the public registry.
  • AMPI Certification: Working with an AMPI-certified advisor like myself means you have a professional bound by ethical standards and ongoing education requirements, providing an additional layer of protection.

What About Healthcare and Infrastructure?

A safe place to invest should also be a safe place to live. Puerto Vallarta offers excellent healthcare infrastructure, including modern private hospitals like CMQ, Joya Hospital, and Hospital San Javier, many with English-speaking staff and internationally trained physicians. The city has reliable public utilities, clean drinking water systems in most developments, and a well-maintained road network.

The recent completion of the Puente Federación (Federation Bridge) has improved connectivity between Puerto Vallarta and the Riviera Nayarit, reducing commute times and further integrating the metropolitan area. Highway improvements and airport expansion projects signal continued government commitment to the region's growth.

My Honest Recommendation

After more than a decade in Puerto Vallarta's real estate market, helping buyers from all walks of life, here's what I can tell you with confidence: Puerto Vallarta is the safest tourist-residential destination in Mexico for property investment.

The data supports it. The expat community proves it. The economic fundamentals reinforce it. And my personal experience — walking these streets every day, raising my family here, building my career here — confirms it.

If you're considering buying property in Puerto Vallarta, I'd love to have a conversation with you. No pressure, no sales pitch — just an honest discussion about your goals, your concerns, and how I can help you make the right decision. That's what I do, and I'm proud of it.
